## Step 2: It's all about choosing the right person
The first thing you need to know is: No matter how good you do or how hard you try.\
If it's ***not the right person***, it ***won't work***.

You have to set strict standars before choosing someone you want to spend a **big** part of your life with or even your **whole** life with.\
Everyone has it's own standards. *(but they are not always good. Some people enjoy toxic relationships...)*

#### Here a gradient of relationships:
A relationship has degree and this degree is linear, the closer you are to a person, the higher they are in the gradient. 

1. Stranger
2. Acquaintance
3. Colleague
4. Friend
5. Close friend
6. Best Friend
7. Lover

So if the person can't validate all the degrees below, then they can't be at a certain degree. *(Example: A person can't be your best friend if you can't see them as a friend, or a just a colleague, etc... It's more relevant for Lover. If a person can't be the other 6 for you, then they can't be your Lover.)*

And I'll also show that the typic relationship we have in this society are crap. *(It feels like a contract you do with a company and it shouldn't)*\
So here are standars you **should** have: *(Not mandatory but it's an awesome example to have a healthy relationship.)*

1. **You should be able to be best friend with the person**
    - If you can't be best friend with them how do you want to be in a serious relationship?\
    What does it mean if you can't be best friend with them? You can't have fun with them? Can share what you share with your friends? You can bring them along with your friends?\
    If you can't be best friend, it's surely a bad idea to be closer. [Refer to the gradient](#here-a-gradient-of-how-relationships-go)

2. **It's not about having someone, it's about caring about the person**
    - If you "love" only because you want a partner because you feel alone or something, it's wrong.\
    You need to actually care about the person, care about their feelings, not just be with them because you want someone close to you but also be with them because you want them because you care about their happiness.\
    If you only care about how you feel and not them, you are selfish and won't realize the harm you are doing to them.

3. **You feel insanely good with them**
    - The person is kind, right or at least trying their best.\
    Being with them is a relief, not a chore and it should always be that way.\
    Sometimes things can be rough because life will throw you challenging situations in the face. But you should always be on the same team and fight for one another.\
    (Note that in the first few weeks/months of the relationship, this feeling of relief is not relevant.\
    It's normal to feel insanely great in the beginning because it's a fresh start, everything is new and at it's best. But it's genuine only if it last after the start phase (3-6 months))
